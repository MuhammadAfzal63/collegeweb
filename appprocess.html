Selection Criteria for Admission to Public Sector Colleges in Punjab Province:


If you want to apply for admission to an intermediate program in a public sector
govt Associate college TMP , you need to follow these general conditions:


  1. You should fill out a specific admission form for the college, based on the program you want
    to study, such as Science, Arts, General Science, or I.C.S.
  2. Sports-based admission quota is only available for F.A. (Arts) programs.
  3. There is a 2% quota for admission based on physical disability for F.A./F.Sc. classes.
    To apply for this quota, you need to attach a disability certificate issued by a recognized institution.
  4. Foreign candidates should send their applications through their Embassy, Consulate,
    or High Commission to the Federal Ministry of Education in Islamabad.

Required Documents:


If your name is placed in the Merit List, you should bring the following original documents
along with photocopies:s


  • Matriculation result card (2 photocopies)
  • Four passport-size photos with a blue background (1.5 x 1.5 size)
  • Character certificate (2 photocopies)
  • Attested photocopy of your father/guardian's CNIC (1 copy)s
  • Attested copy of your CNIC/B-Form (1 copy)
  • Original NOC (No Objection Certificate) if you studied Matric from a board other than
    Punjab Board, along with two photocopies.

Additional Information:


  • Admission is solely based on merit.
  • Parents are responsible for any illegal dealings with college officials, officers, or teachers.
  • Any admission made due to a clerical mistake will be canceled from the beginning.
  • If the college notices unbecoming conduct by an applicant, their application is likely to be rejected.
  • The admission procedure for the second shift intermediate classes follows government rules.

O-Level / Senior Cambridge Students:


  • 4% of seats are reserved for Senior Cambridge / O level students. They should apply for
    admission along with matriculation students.
  • Students studying in Senior Cambridge or equivalent classes must provide detailed
    information about their courses and an equivalence certificate from the Inter Board
    Committee of Chairmen.
  • They must submit their Senior Cambridge Certificate to the college office as soon as their
    exam results are declared. Their numerical grades must meet the college's merit
  • Candidates are expected to pass all their subjects at Senior Cambridge / O-Level.
